What is Broadway?

Broadway refers to the theatrical performances presented in the 41 professional theatres, each with 500 or more seats located in the Theater District and Lincoln Center along Broadway, in Midtown Manhattan, New York City (Read More on Wikipedia.)

Broadway is widely considered to represent the highest level of commercial theatre in the English-speaking world.

Some of the many Broadway Theaters are the: Al Hirschfeld, which plays "Moulin Rouge," August Wilson, which plays "Mean Girls," Brooks Atkinson, which plays "Waitress," Gerald Schoenfeld, which plays my favorite musical, "Come From Away," and Richard Rogers, which plays "Hamilton." There are many other theaters and each of them have their own special details.

Plays Verses Musicals

A Play, by definition is a form of literature written by a playwright, usually consisting of dialogue or singing between characters, intended for theatrical performance rather than just reading. On Broadway, a play is a show that does not include dancing or song.

A Musical is a form of theatrical performance that combines songs, spoken dialogue, acting and dance. Musicals do not always have dance, such as in "Dear Evan Hansen" or "Come from Away," but many musicals cast with dance skill in mind as well. Musicals are the more common of the two on Broadway, but there are still many plays currently running in Broadway theaters.
